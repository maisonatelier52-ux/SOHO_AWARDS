Nomination guide and submission flow

SOHO Awards nominations are evaluated through category eligibility, documentary evidence, verification where required, and jury scoring. A nomination fee of ₹75,000 + applicable taxes applies per submission, and submission does not guarantee selection.

Before you begin
  • Keep registration proof ready: GST, CIN, Udyam, LLP, partnership deed, or equivalent.
  • Collect category-specific evidence such as KPIs, financial summaries, references, or licenses.
  • Prepare authorized signatory details, declarations, and complete the ₹75,000 + applicable taxes nomination fee through the integrated payment flow.
  • Ensure any third-party data or references are being shared lawfully and with authority.
Submission rules
  • One applicant may apply in only one category.
  • One company can receive only one competitive award.
  • A nomination fee of ₹75,000 + applicable taxes applies to each submission and is non-refundable once the file enters administrative processing.
  • Incomplete or unverifiable submissions may be rejected.
  • Sensitive commercial values may be redacted, but key identifiers and proof elements must remain visible.
Selection process

How a nomination moves through the system

  1. Fee paid and application received

    Your submission enters the administrative review queue with the nomination fee reference and is assigned an internal reference.

  2. Completeness review

    The Secretariat checks mandatory fields, declarations, and uploads.

  3. Eligibility check

    The application is assessed against the selected category's public criteria.

  4. Evidence review

    Claims are checked for documentary support, readability, and consistency.

  5. Verification where required

    Clarification calls or reference checks may be run for high-impact or unclear claims.

  6. Jury scoring and moderation

    Eligible submissions are scored using category-specific weighted rubrics before final moderation.

Nomination fee does not affect judging

The nomination fee of ₹75,000 + applicable taxes is a processing charge. It does not improve the chance of shortlisting or winning, and it remains separate from judging, jury scoring, and any optional visibility services.

What judges do not see

Judges do not see sponsorship pricing, winner-activation pricing, or media package sales materials. Commercial conversations are segregated from the evaluation process.
