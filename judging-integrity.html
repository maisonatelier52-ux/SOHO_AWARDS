Judging & Integrity

A recognition platform built on evidence, review, and accountability

Every nomination is evaluated through category eligibility, documentary evidence, verification where required, and jury scoring. The process is designed to be merit-led and commercially independent.

Evidence-based evaluation Conflict-of-interest safeguards Awards may be withheld
Why this page exists

Recognition has value only when the process behind it can withstand scrutiny. That is why this platform does not rely on public voting, popularity, or visibility purchases to determine outcomes.

Evaluation process

How judging works

  1. Nomination submission

    Applicants submit one category application with mandatory documents, declarations, and evidence.

  2. Completeness and eligibility screening

    The Awards Secretariat checks category fit, mandatory documents, and administrative readiness.

  3. Evidence review and sanity check

    Applications are checked for consistency, readability, and basic credibility before they reach the jury.

  4. Verification where required

    Clarification calls, reference checks, or document follow-ups may be run for unclear or high-impact claims.

  5. Jury scoring

    Eligible submissions are scored against category-specific weighted rubrics using a 0–5 rating scale and 100-point total framework.

  6. Final moderation

    Scores, red flags, and verification outcomes are reviewed before recommendations are confirmed.

What judges evaluate
  • Category fit and eligibility
  • Quality and credibility of evidence
  • Measured performance and outcomes
  • Execution discipline
  • Customer, market, or stakeholder impact
  • Governance and integrity signals where relevant
What judges do not score
  • Popularity or public votes
  • Social media reach
  • Sponsorship level
  • Visibility package purchases
  • Claims without supporting proof
  • General reputation unsupported by the submission
Conflict safeguards

Jurors are expected to disclose any conflict of interest. A conflicted juror does not score or influence the outcome of the relevant application.

Commercial independence

Optional visibility, production, sponsor, or media offerings are separate from judging. Purchasing or declining them does not affect selection.
